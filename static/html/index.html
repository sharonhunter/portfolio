d3.js : Unemployment Before and After 2008 Recession - Interactive Map

North and South Carolina, 2006 - 2014

This map shows the unemployment rates for each county for the years before, during, and after the 2008 recession.

From 2000 until 2006 (prior to the recession), unemployment ranged from a low of 3.3% in North Carolina and 3.7% in South Carolina, to a high of 6.9% for North Carolina and a high of 7.0% for South Carolina.

The rates shown on the map range from an overall low of 2.2% through an overall high of 23.5% during the period from 2006 - 2014.

* Data sourced from Bureau of Labor Statistics API, 2006 - 2014. *
